In the OAuth world, Scopes were designed for humans delegating access to apps. They are broad and "bucket-based" because humans don't want to approve 50 individual permissions just to log in.

Better Auth works perfectly with this. It issues a standard Bearer token with a scope: "user" or scope: "admin".

The Reality: For 90% of your Human UI (browsing the dashboard, editing your profile), standard scopes are fine.

2. The "RAR for Everyone" Argument (The Sovereign OS Path)

If you are building a Sovereign Business OS, you treat the Human Dashboard as just another "Agent" that happens to be controlled by a human.

The Logic: When Marcus logs in, the Dashboard app requests a token. Instead of a scope, it requests a Rich Authorization Request (RAR):

JSON
{
  "type": "ui_access",
  "actions": ["read_all", "veto_intents"],
  "locations": ["/dashboard/*"]
}
The Benefit: You have a single token validation logic for the entire OS. Whether it's a Claude-powered sandbox or a React-powered dashboard, the "Brain" checks the RAR object.

Human Authentication (Better Auth): Keep using standard scopes/sessions for the Human login to your website. This is your "Entry Point."
